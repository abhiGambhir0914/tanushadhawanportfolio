Adorn - User Experience

Overview

There is a humongous amount of local talent in INDIA who sell their handmade craftwork in the local market leaving them connected to a small reach of people, provide these talented craftsmen with a solution to connect them with high standard and international outreach.

Problem

There are many sketch artists/sculptors around INDIA and have their portfolios and at the same time there is a very rare group of the people around the world who are fond of hand sketched arts. How can technology help bridge this gap and keep the tradition of art alive.

Objective

To provide local talent with the ease use of technology.

Research & Analysis

User research focuses on understanding user behaviors, needs, and motivations through observation techniques, task analysis, and other feedback methodologies. For me user research is “the process of understanding the impact of design on an audience”.

Personas

With common scenario’s in place, I was able to define detailed personas, using their vast data pool. The personas looked something like this:


Sushila Devi, 24 yr

Bio
Sushila Devi belongs to Bihar and makes eco-friendly products that are commanly used in households accessories and in the form of carry bags, table mats coasters, and some decorative items.
Needs
  • Platform or area to sell products space to set up there work in an area where people tend to buy their products.
  • Raw material to produce product and to maket their products.
  • Difficult to find raw materials and they turn out to be expensive once found.
Goals
  • To be able to sell products in high quantity.
  • To get customers who buy products.
  • To get the rent amount out and plus the profit.
Painpoints
  • Expensive rents for place to sell products.
  • Not being able to find customers in there hometown.
  • Taking products from one place to another in order to sell it.
Tasks
  • Getting raw material to make products.
  • Producing products in quantity and variations.
  • Taking products from one place to another in order to sell them.
Figure 3. Adorn's Personas
Insights
  • Local vendors who produce handmade products tend to just sell the products in local markets.
  • Certain vendors don't have base money to invest in shops.
  • The work they produce is high end since all the products are of their ideation and handmade which adds more value to them.
  • But there less or lack of knowledge about promoting and putting products online on these platforms end up in not engaging these people as sellers.
  • What also happens is that since these people have the difficulty of putting their products online so what mediators do is they buy their products at small prices and then, launch those same products on online platforms for higher pricing.
  • One major problem that these people face is, all the steps involved in creating a seller account. Since they don't have an idea to fill up the description of product details, all these steps end up being time-consuming as well.
